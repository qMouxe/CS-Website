Sir Tim Berners-Lee and the World Wide Web

Here you will find some information about Sir Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the world wide web and the process of how he did it, what technologies he used and the impacts of his invention.

History

The World Wide Web was invented by Sir Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 while he was working at CERN. The world wide web might be confused with the internet. However they are not the same thing. The world wide web are the pages displayed on screen when a device is online. However, the internet is the network of connected computers that the world wide web works on, as well as any data that travels between them.

Technologies he Invented

Sir Tim Berners-Lee had to invent some technologies in order to make his world wide web work. The three fundamentals that he invented are: HTML, URL, and HTTP. HTML is the markup or formatting language for the web. URL or uniform resource locator is a kind of address that is unique to each resource on the web. HTTP stands for hypertext transfer protocol and allows for the retrieval of linked resources from URL's.

Impacts of the World Wide Web

Sir Tim Berners-Lee decided to make his invention royalty-free which means that after an initial payment to use his invention, further uses are free of charge. Apart from bringing the world closer together by making it much easier for people to get information, share and communicate, it also allows people to do this for free. This has led to the world wide web having a strong influence on the economic growth rates of many large and developed economies.
